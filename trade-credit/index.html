Trade Credit Wisdom

💡 6 Rules for Safer Trade Credit

1

Know Your Customer (KYC) Isn't Optional

Request audited financials, trade references, and bank letters. A company that won't share basic financials is a company you shouldn't extend credit to. Check corporate registries in their jurisdiction — it takes 5 minutes and costs nothing.

2

Start Small, Scale Credit

Begin with T/T advance or L/C for the first 3 orders. Once they've proven payment reliability, gradually increase credit terms. A customer who demands open account from Day 1 is a red flag — established buyers understand staged trust.

3

DSO Is Your Early Warning System

Days Sales Outstanding above 60 is a danger zone. If a customer's DSO creeps up month-over-month, freeze shipments until the trend reverses. Late payment isn't just annoying — it's often the first sign of cash flow distress.

4

Country Risk Compounds Everything

A perfect credit score in a high-risk country is still high-risk. Currency controls, political instability, and weak legal systems can turn a "good" customer into a write-off overnight. Always factor country risk separately from company risk.

5

Get Trade Credit Insurance

For anything above $50K exposure, TCI is a no-brainer. Premiums are typically 0.2-1.5% of insured turnover. Compare that to losing an entire $80K shipment. The math is simple. Most insurers also provide credit limit recommendations — use them.

6

Diversify Your Receivables

No single customer should represent more than 20% of your accounts receivable. Concentration risk kills more trading companies than bad products ever do. If one customer going under would sink your business, you're already in trouble.
